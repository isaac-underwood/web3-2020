Make it simple, but significant.

- Don Draper

1.
Full page (scrolling) content.

I love websites that use full page sections that you can scroll between. It allows you to easily integrate full page backgrounds or colours.
The Apple website's product pages integrate full page sections very well. The user's attention is drawn to the content, such as phone specifications, a lot more. Sometimes phone specifications can be a lot for someone to take in. Usability could be affected if a page is not structured well or its content is not engaging.
Example: Apple iPhone 11 Pro

2.
Big. Bold. Typography.

Websites such as RED Company that use large, standout headings and other typography to grab attention or display important information is vital to modern design. Choosing the right fonts for a particular website is equally important. If large headings are overused it could cause users to get lazy and skip over small text that you may want them to read.
Example: RED Company

3.
Whitespace

Whitespace is important so that pages or sections do not feel too cluttered and ulimately, poorly designed. Samsung's S20 page uses whitespace very well by leaving space between elements and sections. It gives a clean and minimalistic look overall.
Example: Samsung Galaxy S20
